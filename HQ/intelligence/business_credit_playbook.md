## 1. Structure Before Credit

---

## 2. Build Business Credit Profile

### Credit Bureaus to Register With
1. **Dun & Bradstreet** — Get D-U-N-S Number
2. **Experian Business**
3. **Equifax Business**

### Starter Vendor Tradelines (Report to Bureaus)
- Uline
- Quill
- Grainger
- Summa Office Supplies

**Strategy:** Order small items, pay early → builds PAYDEX score

---

## 3. Graduate to Revolving Credit

### Business Credit Cards (Report to Business Bureaus)
- AmEx Blue Business Plus
- Chase Ink
- Capital One Spark

### Best Practices
- Keep utilization under 30%
- Pay 5-7 days before due date for max score growth
- Keep business and personal credit separate

---

## 4. Bigger Financing Options

| Type | Use Case |
|------|----------|
| Business lines of credit | Flexible cash flow |
| Equipment financing | Scaling physical operations |
| SBA loans | Big growth capital |
| Community development loans | Local business expansion |

---

## 5. Protect & Expand

- Maintain Net 30/60 vendor ecosystem for cash flow
- Keep personal and business credit completely separate
- As revenue grows, increase credit limits strategically

---

## Mindset

> Business credit isn't just about getting loans—it's about buying **time, leverage, and freedom**. Build a reputation with banks and vendors so you can expand without draining personal cash.

---

## 90-Day Starter Roadmap

| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1-2 | File for D-U-N-S number, open business bank account |
| 3-4 | Order from 2-3 vendor tradelines (Uline, Quill) |
| 5-8 | Pay all invoices early, establish payment history |
| 9-12 | Apply for first business credit card |
| 13+ | Continue building, monitor scores |
